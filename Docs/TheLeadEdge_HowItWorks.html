Chapter 1

The Big Picture

From raw data to your phone in five steps

Right now, finding leads means scrolling through MLS, hoping for referrals, or buying expensive lists of strangers. TheLeadEdge flips this completely. It watches everything — every listing change, every public record filing, every price reduction — and connects the dots to find the people who are most likely to need a Realtor right now.

Here's what happens behind the scenes, every single day:

📥

Step 1: Gather Data

TheLeadEdge pulls fresh data from your MLS — new listings, status changes, price reductions, expirations. It also checks county records for foreclosure filings, probate cases, and tax delinquencies.

🔍

Step 2: Detect Signals

Raw data becomes signals. A price reduction isn't just a number change — it's a signal that the seller is getting anxious. An expired listing after 180 days means an agent failed. A probate filing plus a vacant property means someone inherited a house they probably want to sell.

🎯

Step 3: Score & Rank

Each signal has a point value. Signals on the same property stack together and multiply. The system calculates a 0-100 score and assigns a tier: S (act today), A (48 hours), B (this week), C (monthly), or D (monitor). The score also accounts for freshness — a signal from yesterday is worth more than one from 3 weeks ago.

✉️

Step 4: Deliver Your Briefing

At 6:30 AM, you get an email with your top leads — ranked, explained, and ready for action. Each lead includes why it scored high, what approach to take, and the key details you need.

📱

Step 5: You Take Action

Make your calls, send your CMAs, knock on doors — knowing you're spending your time on the people most likely to say yes. Log the outcome, and the system learns from it to get even smarter over time.

The key insight

You're not getting a list of strangers. You're getting a list of people who are already showing signs they need a Realtor — they just haven't called one yet. You're reaching them at the exact right moment.

Chapter 2

The Leads TheLeadEdge Finds

Six categories of motivated sellers, each with a different approach

⏰

Expired Listings

Their listing expired without selling. They still want to sell — their agent just failed them.
Why they're valuable: They've already committed to selling. They're frustrated with their last agent. They need someone who can explain what went wrong and do it better.
Approach: Market analysis + your track record + what you'll do differently
🏡

FSBOs (For Sale By Owner)

They're trying to sell without an agent. Most get frustrated within 3-6 weeks.
Why they're valuable: They've already decided to sell. They just need to be shown the value an agent brings — especially when their price keeps dropping.
Approach: Recent comps + "what an agent could net you" + no pressure
⚠️

Pre-Foreclosure

A foreclosure notice has been filed. The homeowner is in financial distress and often has a deadline to sell.
Why they're valuable: Extremely motivated by timeline. Selling before foreclosure saves their credit and often lets them walk away with equity.
Approach: Empathy first, always. Options counseling. Never pushy. Be the person who helps.
📜

Probate / Estate

Someone passed away and left a property. The heir usually lives far away and wants to sell.
Why they're valuable: High motivation to sell (they don't want to manage a remote property). But requires patience and sensitivity — they're grieving.
Approach: Compassion + estate process guidance + patience. Follow up gently over months.
📉

Price Reductions

Active listings that keep dropping their price. Each reduction signals growing anxiety.
Why they're valuable: They're still listed with an agent, but the agent may be losing their confidence. Multiple drops = the seller is getting desperate.
Approach: "I noticed your listing — here's what the market is telling us" + your strategy
🔄

Withdrawn & Relisted

A listing was pulled off the market and put back on — often with a new agent or new price.
Why they're valuable: The first attempt failed. They're still motivated to sell but now more cautious. They want an agent who gets it right.
Approach: Acknowledge the frustration + fresh market analysis + what went wrong before

You choose which types to pursue

Not comfortable with pre-foreclosure? That's completely fine. TheLeadEdge lets you turn any lead type on or off. You only see what you want to see.

Chapter 8

Common Questions

Everything you might be wondering

Do I have to learn new software?
Barely. Phase 1 is just email — you read a briefing, same as any other email. The dashboard (Phase 3) is a simple website you open in your browser. If you can use Zillow, you can use TheLeadEdge.
What if I don't like the suggested scripts?
They're suggestions, not requirements. The scripts are frameworks that match each lead's specific situation. You personalize them with your own voice. Over time, we'll tune them to match how YOU actually talk to people.
Is this legal? What about Do Not Call lists?
Yes, 100% legal. TheLeadEdge has compliance built into every layer. DNC lists are checked automatically. CAN-SPAM rules are followed for emails. State-specific waiting periods (like for foreclosure outreach) are enforced in code. You can't accidentally break the rules.
What if the system gives me too many leads?
The scoring thresholds are adjustable. If you're getting 20 S-tier leads a day (you won't — that would be amazing), we raise the bar. The goal is a manageable daily number that matches your capacity. You tell us what feels right.
What about my MLS terms of service?
TheLeadEdge uses your authorized MLS access — the same data you already have permission to see. We never scrape, redistribute, or share MLS data. The system just helps you analyze what you're already looking at, faster and smarter.
What happens to the leads I don't contact?
They stay in your pipeline. Their scores decay over time (because urgency fades), but if new signals appear — another price drop, a new public record — the score goes back up and they reappear in your briefing. Nothing falls through the cracks.
Can I use this on my phone?
Yes. The email briefing works on any phone. The dashboard (Phase 3) is built as a mobile-friendly web app — works in Safari or Chrome, no app store download needed.
What if I stop using it for a while?
No penalty, no contracts, no wasted data. The system just keeps watching. When you come back, your pipeline is still there, and the most current leads are waiting for you. And if you stop using it entirely, we redesign it around what you actually need.
How is this different from buying leads on Zillow?
Zillow leads are people who clicked a button — they're being sent to 3-5 agents simultaneously, and you're competing on speed. TheLeadEdge leads are people showing behavioral signals of motivation that only you can see. You're the only agent who knows about them. No competition, higher conversion.
Does this replace my CRM?
No — it feeds your CRM. TheLeadEdge finds and scores the leads. When you decide to pursue one, it pushes the lead to your CRM (Follow Up Boss, etc.) with all the context and a suggested action plan. Your CRM handles the ongoing relationship.
