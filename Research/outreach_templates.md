## 1. Brand Voice & General Principles

### The Voice

Every piece of outreach should sound like it comes from a knowledgeable neighbor who genuinely wants to help -- not a salesperson reading from a script. The Realtor is a woman who is:

- **Confident** without being overbearing
- **Knowledgeable** without being condescending
- **Empathetic** without being pitying
- **Direct** without being pushy
- **Warm** without being overly casual

### Universal Rules for All Outreach

1. **Lead with value, not with your need for a listing.** Every contact should offer something useful -- a market insight, a CMA, a resource, a connection -- before asking for anything.
2. **Never bash another agent.** Even if the previous agent objectively failed, criticizing them insults the homeowner's judgment. Focus on what you would do differently.
3. **Personalize everything.** Generic mass messaging is the fastest way to get ignored. Reference the specific property, neighborhood, or situation.
4. **Respect the "no."** If someone asks to be removed from contact, do it immediately. One polite request honored is worth more than ten forced touches.
5. **Provide an easy exit.** Every email and letter should include a clear, guilt-free way to opt out or decline.
6. **Be truthful.** Never imply you have a buyer when you do not. Never inflate market values. Never promise outcomes you cannot control.
7. **Document everything.** Log every contact, every response, every opt-out. This protects you legally and helps you refine your approach.

### Channel Selection Guide

| Lead Type | Best Initial Channel | Why |
|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| Expired Listings | Email/Phone + CMA delivery | Speed matters; same-day contact wins |
| FSBO | Phone or door visit | Personal connection breaks through |
| Pre-Foreclosure | Direct mail (letter) | Less invasive; legal considerations |
| Probate | Direct mail (letter) | Sensitivity demands written, non-intrusive approach |
| Divorce | Direct mail (letter) | Privacy concerns; no phone unless invited |
| Price Reduction Stacking | Email or phone | Active listing = active engagement |
| High DOM | Email with market data | Data-driven approach feels consultative |
| Withdrawn & Relisted | Phone or email | Timely; they are actively reconsidering |
| Absentee Owners | Direct mail + email | Must reach them at their mailing address |
| Long-Term Owners | Direct mail (handwritten note) | Personal touch stands out in this segment |
| Past Clients | Phone + email | Warm relationship; personal connection |
| Referral Partners | Email + in-person meeting | Professional relationship building |

## 2. Expired Listings

### Key Value Proposition

"Your home deserved better marketing. I have a specific plan to reach buyers your previous listing may have missed -- and I have the data to show why the market is on your side right now."

### Best Contact Channel

Phone + immediate CMA delivery (email or hand-delivered). Speed is critical -- contact within hours of expiration, not days.

### Initial Contact -- Phone Script (Day 0)

> Hi [OWNER_NAME], this is [AGENT_NAME] with [BROKERAGE]. I know you are probably getting a lot of calls right now, so I will be brief and respectful of your time.
>
> I noticed your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] came off the market, and I wanted to reach out -- not to give you a sales pitch, but because I put together a market analysis for your property that I think you would find genuinely useful.
>
> I have been tracking buyer activity in [NEIGHBORHOOD] closely, and there are some things happening in the market right now that I think work in your favor. Would it be alright if I sent that analysis over to you? There is absolutely no obligation -- I just think you would find the information valuable as you decide your next steps.

### Initial Contact -- Email (Day 0)

**Subject Lines** (choose one):

- Your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] -- a fresh market perspective
- [NEIGHBORHOOD] buyer activity update for [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]
- I put together something for [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] -- no strings attached
- Market analysis for [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] -- some encouraging data

**Body:**

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I know this is not the outcome you were hoping for with your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS], and I imagine you are weighing your options right now.
>
> I took some time to put together a Comparative Market Analysis for your property -- not because I am trying to add to the noise, but because I noticed some market trends in [NEIGHBORHOOD] that I think you would find encouraging.
>
> Here is what stood out to me:
>
> - [NUMBER] homes similar to yours have sold in [NEIGHBORHOOD] in the last [TIMEFRAME]
> - The average sale price in your area has [increased/held steady] by [PERCENTAGE] over the past [TIMEFRAME]
> - There are currently [NUMBER] active buyers in my network looking in your price range and area
>
> I have attached the full analysis for you. Whether you decide to relist or take some time off the market, I think this information will be helpful as you plan your next move.
>
> If you would like to talk through it, I am happy to do that -- no pressure and no obligation. And if you would rather not hear from me again, just let me know and I will respect that completely.
>
> Warm regards,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE]
> [EMAIL]
> [BROKERAGE]

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Phone + Email | CMA delivery (above) |
| 2 | Day 3 | Email | "Following up on the CMA I sent -- did you have a chance to look it over? Happy to walk through it." |
| 3 | Day 7 | Email or mail | Neighborhood activity update: recent sales, pending sales, new listings nearby |
| 4 | Day 14 | Phone or door knock | "I wanted to share a marketing plan I put together specifically for your home" |
| 5 | Day 30 | Mail | Handwritten note: "Still thinking of you and your home at [ADDRESS]. When the time feels right, I am here." |
| 6 | Day 60-90 | Email | Monthly market update for their neighborhood; soft check-in |

### Touch 2 -- Follow-Up Email (Day 3)

**Subject:** Quick follow-up on the market analysis for [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I just wanted to follow up on the market analysis I sent over a few days ago for [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]. I hope you had a chance to take a look.
>
> If any of the numbers surprised you -- or if you have questions about what is happening in [NEIGHBORHOOD] right now -- I would love to chat. Sometimes a fresh perspective is all it takes to see a path forward.
>
> No pressure at all. I am here whenever you are ready.
>
> Best,
> [AGENT_NAME]

### Touch 3 -- Neighborhood Activity Email (Day 7)

**Subject:** What just happened in [NEIGHBORHOOD] this week

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I wanted to share some quick updates from your neighborhood:
>
> - [ADDRESS_1] just went under contract at [PRICE] after [DOM] days
> - [ADDRESS_2] listed at [PRICE] -- [brief comparison to their home]
> - [NUMBER] new buyer inquiries came in for [NEIGHBORHOOD] this week
>
> The market in your area is [active/moving/showing strength], and I think that is worth knowing as you consider your options.
>
> As always, I am here if you want to talk through anything.
>
> [AGENT_NAME]

### What NOT to Say

- "I see your listing expired" -- they know. Do not state the obvious as your opening.
- "Your previous agent did not market it well" -- never criticize the previous agent directly.
- "I could have sold it" -- presumptuous and unverifiable.
- "You must be frustrated" -- do not project emotions onto them.
- "I have a buyer" -- unless you genuinely do. This is the most common lie in real estate and homeowners know it.
- "What went wrong?" -- puts them on the defensive. Instead ask, "What would you want to see done differently next time?"

### Compliance Notes

- **Do Not Call Registry**: Scrub all phone numbers against the National DNC list before calling. Expired listings are not exempt from DNC rules unless there is a pre-existing business relationship.
- **MLS Data Usage**: Verify that your MLS Terms of Use allow you to contact homeowners whose listings have expired. Most do, but some restrict solicitation of other agents' expired listings for a cooling-off period.
- **CAN-SPAM**: All emails must include your physical address, a clear unsubscribe mechanism, and honest subject lines.
- **State Licensing**: Your outreach must include your real estate license information as required by your state.
- **Time Restrictions**: Do not call before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM in the contact's local time (TCPA requirement).

## 3. FSBO (For Sale By Owner)

### Key Value Proposition

"You clearly know your home and your market. I would love to share some data that could help you maximize your sale -- whether you work with an agent or not."

### Best Contact Channel

Phone call or in-person visit. FSBOs are typically hands-on people who respect direct, practical communication. Email is a secondary channel.

### Initial Contact -- Phone Script

> Hi [OWNER_NAME], this is [AGENT_NAME] with [BROKERAGE]. I saw your home listed for sale at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] -- it is a great property.
>
> I am not calling to convince you that you need an agent -- you are clearly capable of managing the sale yourself. What I wanted to offer is a set of market data for [NEIGHBORHOOD] that I think could be genuinely useful to you, regardless of how you choose to sell.
>
> It includes recent comparable sales, what buyers in this price range are looking for right now, and a few insights on pricing strategy that might help you net more at the closing table. I am happy to send it over by email or drop it off -- no obligation and no follow-up unless you want it.
>
> Would that be helpful?

### Initial Contact -- Email

**Subject Lines** (choose one):

- Market data for your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] -- free, no strings
- [NUMBER] comparable sales near [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] you should know about
- A few insights for your FSBO at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]
- Selling on your own? Here is some data that might help

**Body:**

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I saw your home for sale at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] and wanted to say -- it looks great. You clearly know your property well.
>
> I work extensively in [NEIGHBORHOOD], and I put together some market data that I thought might be useful to you as you navigate the sale:
>
> - [NUMBER] comparable homes have sold in the last [TIMEFRAME], ranging from [PRICE_LOW] to [PRICE_HIGH]
> - Average days on market in your area is currently [DOM_AVERAGE]
> - Buyers in this price range are currently looking for [KEY_FEATURE_1] and [KEY_FEATURE_2]
>
> I know working with an agent may not be your plan, and I respect that completely. But if you ever want a second opinion on pricing, staging, or negotiation strategy, I am happy to be a resource -- even if we never work together formally.
>
> Wishing you a smooth sale,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE]
> [EMAIL]

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Phone + email | Value-first market data offer (above) |
| 2 | Day 10 | Email | "How is the sale going? Here is an update on what just sold near you." |
| 3 | Day 21 | Phone | "Checking in -- has anything changed? I am here if you need a sounding board." |
| 4 | Day 35 | Email | Share a relevant article about FSBO negotiation tips or staging ideas |
| 5 | Day 50+ | Email | "I noticed your home is still on the market. If you would ever like to explore your options, I have some ideas that might help. No pressure." |

### Touch 2 -- FSBO Check-In (Day 10)

**Subject:** How is the sale going at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]?

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I wanted to check in and see how things are going with your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]. Selling on your own takes real effort, and I hope it is going well.
>
> A quick update from [NEIGHBORHOOD]: [ADDRESS_NEARBY] just sold for [PRICE], which is a [positive/neutral] sign for your property.
>
> If you are running into any challenges -- whether it is buyer inquiries that go nowhere, lowball offers, or just wanting a second opinion on something -- I am happy to help. No strings attached.
>
> Best,
> [AGENT_NAME]

### Touch 5 -- Extended Market Time (Day 50+)

**Subject:** A thought on your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]

> Hi [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I noticed your home at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] is still available, and I wanted to share a thought -- not a pitch, just an observation.
>
> Homes in [NEIGHBORHOOD] are currently averaging [DOM_AVERAGE] days on market. When a property goes beyond that window, buyers sometimes start to wonder why, even if the home itself is perfectly priced and well-maintained. It is a perception issue, not a reflection of your home.
>
> There are some strategies -- from refreshing the listing photos to adjusting the exposure channels -- that can reset buyer interest without changing your price. I would be happy to share a few ideas if that would be useful.
>
> Either way, I wish you the best with the sale.
>
> [AGENT_NAME]

### What NOT to Say

- "You need an agent" -- this immediately creates resistance. Let them come to that conclusion.
- "You are leaving money on the table" -- condescending, even if true.
- "Buyers' agents will not show your home" -- this is often repeated but sounds like a scare tactic. Instead, explain how buyer agent cooperation works if asked.
- "Statistics show FSBOs sell for less" -- even if accurate, leading with this feels like you are undermining their decision.
- "I have a buyer for your home" -- only if you genuinely do. This is the oldest trick and FSBOs have heard it before.
- Anything that implies they cannot do this without you -- respect their autonomy.

### Compliance Notes

- **Do Not Call**: FSBOs who list their number publicly on a for-sale sign or advertisement are generally considered to have given implied consent for real estate-related calls. However, if they are on the DNC list and have not listed a contact number publicly, do not call.
- **FSBO Property Rights**: The owner has the right to sell without representation. Do not pressure or suggest they are making a mistake.
- **Fair Housing**: All outreach must be consistent regardless of the neighborhood or owner demographics. Do not selectively target or avoid based on protected classes.
- **State-Specific Rules**: Some states require agents to disclose their license status in all initial contacts, including casual conversations at the property.

## 4. Pre-Foreclosure / NOD

### Key Value Proposition

"You have more options than you might think right now, and time is your most valuable asset. I help homeowners in your situation explore every path to protect their equity and their future."

### Best Contact Channel

Direct mail -- physical letter in a plain envelope with no exterior indication of the contents. This is the most legally safe and empathetically appropriate channel. Never use postcards (visible content is often illegal for foreclosure-related outreach).

### Initial Contact -- Letter (Mailed)

> Dear [OWNER_NAME],
>
> My name is [AGENT_NAME], and I am a licensed Realtor with [BROKERAGE] here in [CITY/AREA]. I am writing because I work with homeowners who are navigating challenging financial situations, and I wanted to make sure you know about the options that may be available to you.
>
> If you are considering a change with your property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS], there are several paths worth exploring:
>
> - **Selling at market value** to preserve your equity and protect your credit
> - **Loan modification or forbearance** through your lender (I can share resources for this)
> - **Short sale**, if the situation calls for it -- I have experience guiding homeowners through this process
>
> I am not here to pressure you or make assumptions about your situation. I simply want you to know that there is someone in your corner who understands the timeline involved and can help you evaluate your options -- confidentially and at no cost for the consultation.
>
> If it would be helpful to talk, you can reach me at [PHONE] or [EMAIL]. Everything we discuss is completely confidential.
>
> With care,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [LICENSE_NUMBER]
> [BROKERAGE]
> [PHONE]
> [EMAIL]

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Week 1 | Letter | Initial outreach (above) |
| 2 | Week 3 | Letter | CMA offer: "I prepared a market analysis for your property -- would you like a copy?" |
| 3 | Week 5 | Letter | Timeline awareness: "I want to make sure you are aware of the options timeline for your situation" |
| 4 | Week 8 | Letter | Resource letter: list of HUD counselors, legal aid, and community resources |
| 5 | Week 12 | Letter | Final soft touch: "I am still here. No judgment, no pressure. Just help when you need it." |

### Touch 2 -- CMA Offer Letter (Week 3)

> Dear [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I reached out a couple of weeks ago, and I wanted to follow up -- not to pressure you, but to offer something concrete.
>
> I have prepared a Comparative Market Analysis for your property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]. This is a detailed report showing what your home is likely worth in today's market, based on recent sales of similar homes nearby.
>
> Why this matters: if you have equity in your home, a market-rate sale could be a way to preserve that equity and move forward on your own terms. Many homeowners in similar situations are surprised to learn how much equity they have built, especially given how the market has performed in [NEIGHBORHOOD] over the past several years.
>
> If you would like me to send you this analysis -- or if you would simply like to talk through your options with someone who understands the process -- please do not hesitate to reach out.
>
> Confidentially yours,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE] | [EMAIL]

### Touch 4 -- Resource Letter (Week 8)

> Dear [OWNER_NAME],
>
> I want to make sure you have access to every resource available to you. Regardless of whether we ever work together, the following organizations provide free and confidential help to homeowners navigating difficult situations:
>
> - **HUD-Approved Housing Counseling**: [LOCAL_HUD_COUNSELOR] or call 1-800-569-4287
> - **[STATE] Legal Aid**: [LOCAL_LEGAL_AID_CONTACT]
> - **[LOCAL_NONPROFIT]**: [DESCRIPTION AND CONTACT]
>
> These are free services -- no one will try to sell you anything.
>
> If you also want to understand what your home could sell for in today's market, I remain happy to provide that information confidentially. Sometimes knowing the numbers can make the path forward clearer.
>
> Thinking of you,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE] | [EMAIL]

### What NOT to Say

- "Foreclosure" on the exterior of any mail piece -- this is illegal in many jurisdictions and always unethical.
- "I can stop your foreclosure" -- this violates the FTC's Mortgage Assistance Relief Services (MARS) Rule and is a misrepresentation of what you can do as a Realtor.
- "You are going to lose your home" -- fear-based language is unethical and counterproductive.
- "I know about your financial situation" -- do not reference the NOD filing directly. Use indirect language like "navigating a transition" or "changes with your property."
- "Time is running out" -- even if true, this reads as a pressure tactic. Instead, share the general timeline for the process and let them draw their own conclusions.
- "This is your only option" -- never position selling as the only path. Always mention loan modification, forbearance, and counseling alongside it.

### Compliance Notes

- **MARS Rule (FTC)**: You cannot claim to be able to stop, prevent, or cure a foreclosure. You are offering a market-rate sale as one of several options.
- **State Foreclosure Solicitation Laws**: Many states restrict when and how you can contact homeowners in foreclosure. California (CC 2945), New York, Illinois, and others have specific statutes. Check your state's requirements.
- **Cooling-Off Periods**: Some states impose a waiting period after the NOD is filed before solicitation is permitted (e.g., California requires 5 business days after NOD recording).
- **Foreclosure Consultant Laws**: In many states, a licensed Realtor offering to list a property at fair market value is exempt from "foreclosure consultant" restrictions, but verify with your broker and state regulatory body.
- **Mail Content**: Never reference "foreclosure," "default," or "delinquency" on the exterior of any envelope. Interior language should be empathetic and service-oriented.
- **DNC Compliance**: Pre-foreclosure homeowners are frequently on the DNC list. Direct mail is strongly preferred over phone outreach for this reason.
- **Record Keeping**: Document every mailing -- date, contents, recipient -- in case of regulatory inquiry.

## 5. Probate / Inherited Property

### Key Value Proposition

"I understand this is a difficult time. If the estate includes real property that needs to be handled, I can take that burden off your plate and guide you through every step -- with patience and care."

### Best Contact Channel

Direct mail -- formal letter to the personal representative (executor/administrator) at their mailing address, sent no sooner than 30 days after the death (90-day waiting period is the ethical standard for the initial outreach; 30 days minimum).

### Initial Contact -- Letter

> Dear [EXECUTOR_NAME],
>
> My name is [AGENT_NAME], and I am a Realtor with [BROKERAGE] in [CITY/AREA]. I am writing because I specialize in helping families navigate the real estate aspects of estate settlement.
>
> I understand that managing an estate involves a great deal -- legally, emotionally, and logistically. If the estate includes real property that may need to be sold, I want you to know that I am available to help in whatever way is useful, whenever you are ready.
>
> What I can offer:
>
> - A **complimentary market valuation** of the property for estate purposes (useful for probate court filings)
> - Guidance on **preparing an inherited property for sale** without costly renovations
> - Coordination with estate attorneys, title companies, and any other professionals involved
> - **Patience** -- there is no timeline pressure from my end. I work on your schedule.
>
> I have helped [NUMBER] families in [AREA] with estate property sales, and I understand the unique requirements of probate transactions, including court confirmation if required in your jurisdiction.
>
> If it would be helpful to talk -- even just to understand your options -- please feel free to reach out at any time.
>
> With sincere sympathy,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [LICENSE_NUMBER]
> [BROKERAGE]
> [PHONE]
> [EMAIL]

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | 30-90 days post-death | Letter | Initial outreach (above) |
| 2 | 3-4 weeks later | Letter | Gentle follow-up with CMA offer and estate sale checklist |
| 3 | 6-8 weeks later | Letter or email | Share a relevant resource: "Guide to Selling Inherited Property" |
| 4 | 3 months later | Letter | Seasonal market update with soft check-in |
| 5 | 6 months later | Letter or email | Final touch: "Still here if you need anything. No expiration on my offer to help." |

### Touch 2 -- Follow-Up with Checklist (3-4 Weeks After Touch 1)

> Dear [EXECUTOR_NAME],
>
> I hope this note finds you well. I reached out a few weeks ago regarding the property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS], and I wanted to follow up with something I hope you will find practical.
>
> I have put together a brief checklist of things to consider when an estate includes real property -- from securing the home and maintaining insurance, to understanding tax implications and preparing for a potential sale. I have enclosed it with this letter.
>
> I have also prepared a preliminary market valuation for the property, which may be useful for your probate filings. I am happy to send that along if you would like -- just let me know.
>
> There is absolutely no urgency from my end. Estates take time, and I am here whenever you are ready to have a conversation.
>
> Warmly,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE] | [EMAIL]

### Touch 5 -- Long-Term Follow-Up (6 Months)

**Subject (if email):** Still here for you -- [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]

> Dear [EXECUTOR_NAME],
>
> It has been several months since I first reached out about the property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS], and I simply wanted to let you know that my offer to help stands -- whenever the timing is right for you.
>
> Estates move at their own pace, and there is no pressure from my side. If and when you are ready to discuss the property, I would be honored to assist.
>
> Wishing you and your family well,
> [AGENT_NAME]

### What NOT to Say

- "Sorry for your loss" as a throwaway opener followed by a sales pitch -- if you express sympathy, let it stand on its own before transitioning to business.
- "The property is just sitting there" -- implies they are being negligent.
- "You should sell before the market changes" -- urgency-based language is inappropriate in grief situations.
- "I specialize in probate" in a way that sounds clinical or transactional -- instead, say "I help families with estate property."
- "What do you plan to do with the house?" as an opening question -- let them volunteer their plans.
- "The property needs work" -- they may not be ready to hear criticism of a loved one's home.
- Any language that implies you are monitoring death records or court filings as a business activity.

### Compliance Notes

- **Ethical Waiting Period**: Industry best practice is to wait at least 30 days after a death before any outreach, and many experienced agents recommend 60-90 days. Some states or local Realtor associations mandate specific waiting periods.
- **Probate Court Rules**: Some jurisdictions require court confirmation of real property sales within an estate. Know your local requirements and be prepared to guide the executor through the process.
- **Fair Housing**: Outreach must be consistent regardless of the decedent's or executor's demographics.
- **Privacy**: Do not reference how you learned about the death or the estate filing. If asked, you can say you monitor public records as part of your practice.
- **Sensitivity Training**: Consider taking a grief-informed communication course. It is not required, but it differentiates your approach.
- **Address Accuracy**: Mail to the executor at their personal mailing address (found in probate filings), not to the decedent at the property address.

## 6. Divorce-Related

### Key Value Proposition

"I understand that real estate decisions during a divorce need to be handled efficiently, fairly, and privately. I can help both parties get a clear picture of the property's value and manage the sale so you can each move forward."

### Best Contact Channel

Direct mail -- letter to the property address. Do not call unless you have been referred by an attorney or the individual has reached out to you first. Privacy is paramount.

### Initial Contact -- Letter

> Dear [OWNER_NAME],
>
> My name is [AGENT_NAME], and I am a Realtor with [BROKERAGE] in [CITY/AREA]. I am writing because I work with homeowners who are going through life transitions that involve real estate decisions.
>
> If you are evaluating what to do with your property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS], I want you to know that I provide confidential, no-obligation market valuations that can be useful whether you are planning to sell, refinance, or simply need an accurate value for legal or financial purposes.
>
> What you can expect from me:
>
> - **Neutrality** -- I work in the best interest of whoever I represent, and I maintain professional boundaries throughout
> - **Privacy** -- everything we discuss is completely confidential
> - **Efficiency** -- I understand that you may want to move through this process as smoothly and quickly as possible
> - **Clarity** -- I will give you straightforward, data-backed answers about your property's value and your options
>
> If a property valuation or a conversation about your options would be helpful, I am available at your convenience.
>
> Respectfully,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [LICENSE_NUMBER]
> [BROKERAGE]
> [PHONE]
> [EMAIL]

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Initial | Letter | Confidential valuation offer (above) |

| 3 | 6-8 weeks | Letter | Market update for their property's neighborhood |
| 4 | 3 months | Letter | "If your situation has evolved, I am here to help whenever you are ready" |

>

>
> Respectfully,
> [AGENT_NAME]
> [PHONE] | [EMAIL]

### What NOT to Say

- "I know you are going through a divorce" -- never reference the divorce directly unless they bring it up. Use neutral language like "life transition" or "change in circumstances."
- "I can help both of you" -- this creates a dual-agency concern. Clarify that you would represent one party or offer a valuation that both parties can use.
- "You need to sell quickly" -- do not assume urgency or create pressure. The timeline is theirs to set.
- "Your spouse contacted me" -- even if true, do not share this unless specifically authorized.
- Anything that takes sides or implies one party is more reasonable than the other.
- "I saw your divorce filing" -- never reference how you found them. If asked, say you reach out to homeowners in transition as part of your practice.

### Compliance Notes

- **Dual Agency**: In most states, you cannot represent both parties in a divorce-related sale without explicit informed consent from both parties (and even then, it is inadvisable). Be clear about who you represent.
- **Privacy Laws**: Divorce filings are public records in most jurisdictions, but referencing them directly in outreach can be perceived as invasive. Use indirect language.
- **Do Not Call**: Do not call unless you have been given the number directly by the homeowner or a referring attorney. Divorce filings on the DNC list should not be called.
- **Court Orders**: If the property is subject to a court order (restraining order, temporary use order), the sale cannot proceed without court permission. Be aware of this possibility.
- **Attorney Involvement**: Many divorce-related sales involve attorneys. Be prepared to coordinate with legal counsel and never give legal advice.
- **Fair Housing**: All outreach must be consistent and non-discriminatory.
- **Mail Sensitivity**: Send mail in a plain, sealed envelope with no references to divorce, family court, or personal circumstances on the exterior.

### Relationship Maintenance After Initial Connection

Once a referral partner relationship is established:

- Send quarterly market updates relevant to their clients
- Share closings or success stories (anonymized) that demonstrate your competence
- Refer business to them when you encounter clients who need their services (reciprocity is the foundation)
- Remember professional milestones (practice anniversaries, awards) and personal ones (birthdays, if shared)
- Host an annual "professional partners" appreciation event or dinner

### What NOT to Say

- "I will pay you a referral fee" -- this is illegal in most states between a Realtor and a non-licensed professional. Never offer compensation for referrals from attorneys, financial advisors, or HR contacts.
- "I am the best agent in town" -- let your work speak for itself. Referral partners care about reliability, not boasting.
- "I can guarantee a quick sale" -- do not make promises you cannot control.
- "I will make it worth your while" -- implies quid pro quo. Focus on the value to their clients, not to them.
- "Your clients need me" -- position yourself as a resource, not a necessity.

### Compliance Notes

- **RESPA (Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act)**: It is illegal to pay kickbacks or referral fees to non-licensed persons for referrals of settlement services (including real estate brokerage). You cannot offer compensation -- monetary or in-kind -- to attorneys, financial advisors, HR professionals, or others for sending you clients.
- **State Bar Rules**: Attorneys are governed by their own ethical rules regarding referrals and compensation. Do not put them in a compromising position.
- **Reciprocal Referrals**: You CAN refer your clients to these professionals when appropriate. Organic, mutual referral relationships built on trust and competence are ethical and encouraged.
- **Marketing Materials**: Providing branded marketing materials (pens, notepads, etc.) to referral partners may be acceptable, but large gifts or incentives could violate RESPA. Check with your broker.
- **Testimonials**: If a referral partner provides a testimonial, ensure they have given written permission and that the testimonial complies with your state's advertising rules.

## 17. Voicemail Script

A single, versatile voicemail script that can be adapted for any lead type. The goal: create curiosity and provide value, not pressure.

### Universal Voicemail Script

> Hi [OWNER_NAME], this is [AGENT_NAME] with [BROKERAGE]. I am calling because I put together some market information for your property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] that I think you would find really valuable.
>
> I work closely in [NEIGHBORHOOD], and I noticed some [interesting activity / encouraging trends / recent sales] nearby that are worth knowing about -- whether you are thinking about selling or just like staying informed.
>
> I would love to share it with you. You can reach me at [PHONE] -- that is [PHONE REPEATED]. Or feel free to text or email me at [EMAIL].
>
> Again, this is [AGENT_NAME] with [BROKERAGE]. I hope to hear from you, but either way, I hope you have a great [day/evening]. Take care.

### Key Principles for Voicemail

- **Under 30 seconds**. People stop listening after that.
- **Say your name and number twice** -- once at the beginning and once at the end.
- **Create curiosity**, not urgency: "I found some interesting data" is better than "Call me back as soon as possible."
- **Offer value**: Reference something specific you have prepared for them.
- **Sound like a human**, not a script. Smile when you speak -- it changes your tone.
- **Never leave more than one voicemail per week** for the same person.
- **Do not leave details about sensitive situations** (foreclosure, divorce, probate) in a voicemail. Simply say "I have some information that might be helpful."

### Voicemail Variations by Lead Type

**Expired Listing:**
> ...I noticed your home came off the market, and I put together a fresh market analysis that highlights some buyer activity in your area that I think you will find encouraging...

**FSBO:**
> ...I saw your home for sale and I have some comparable sales data from [NEIGHBORHOOD] that might be useful to you as you navigate the sale -- whether you work with an agent or not...

**Absentee Owner:**
> ...I work in the neighborhood where you own property, and I wanted to share some recent market activity near your home that could be relevant to your investment...

**Past Client:**
> ...It has been a while since we worked together and I have been thinking about you. I have some great news about what has been happening in your neighborhood -- give me a call when you get a chance!

**Pre-Foreclosure / Probate / Divorce (Sensitive):**
> ...I have some information about options available for your property at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] that I think you would find helpful. I can be reached at [PHONE] anytime, and everything is completely confidential.

### Compliance Notes

- **TCPA**: Voicemails left on cell phones are subject to TCPA rules. Do not use autodialed or pre-recorded messages to cell phones without prior express consent.
- **DNC**: Verify the number is not on the Do Not Call list before calling.
- **Time Restrictions**: Call only between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM in the recipient's local time zone.
- **Caller ID**: Do not block or spoof your caller ID. Your real phone number and/or brokerage number must be displayed.
- **Recording Laws**: Some states are "two-party consent" states for call recording. If you record calls for training, be aware of your state's requirements.

## 18. Compliance Master Reference

### Federal Regulations

| Law | What It Covers | Key Requirements |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| **Do Not Call (DNC) Registry** | Telemarketing calls | Check all numbers against the registry before calling. Existing business relationships are exempt for 18 months after last transaction. |
| **CAN-SPAM Act** | Commercial email | Must include physical address, unsubscribe mechanism, honest subject lines. Process opt-outs within 10 business days. |
| **TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)** | Phone calls and texts | No auto-dialed calls or pre-recorded messages to cell phones without prior express consent. No calls before 8 AM or after 9 PM local time. |

| **RESPA** | Settlement service referrals | No kickbacks or referral fees between real estate licensees and non-licensed professionals (attorneys, financial advisors, etc.). |
| **MARS Rule (FTC)** | Foreclosure-related solicitation | Cannot claim to stop or prevent foreclosure. Cannot collect advance fees for foreclosure assistance. |

- **Email marketing rules**: Some states have stricter anti-spam laws than CAN-SPAM
- **Probate solicitation rules**: Waiting periods, restrictions on contacting executors
- **Privacy laws**: California (CCPA), Virginia, Colorado, and others have consumer privacy laws that may impact data usage
